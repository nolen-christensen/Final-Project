The Sport of Golf

A personal beginner's insight into the sport that demands precision, patience, and passion.

My Love for the Game

Golf is more than just a sport. It's a lifestyle, a mental challenge, and a deeply rewarding pursuit. I picked up a club for the first time when I was a young teen, and am starting to regain my passion for the game after years on the sidelines. Whether it's the early-morning dew on a quiet fairway or the satisfying crack of a well-struck iron, there's nothing quite like a round of golf to clear the mind and sharpen focus.

Why Everyone Should Try Golf

Golf offers benefits that go far beyond the scorecard. Here are the top reasons to give it a shot:

  1. Mental Benefits
    • Improves focus and concentration under pressure
    • Teaches patience as you can't rush a good swing
    • Provides a genuine mental reset away from screens
    • Builds resilience after a bad shot or bad hole
  2. Physical Benefits
    • Walking 18 holes = 4–6 miles of low-impact exercise
    • Builds core strength, balance, and flexibility
    • Fresh air and sunlight boost vitamin D levels
  3. Social Benefits
    • Outstanding networking environment
    • Quality time with friends and family across all ages
    • Tournaments and club events foster community
  4. Lifelong Sport
    • Can be played competitively or recreationally at any age
    • Handicap system levels the playing field between skill levels
    • Courses around the world provide endless new experiences

Golf Essentials for Beginners

Getting started doesn't have to be expensive. Here's what you actually need:

A basic set of clubs is all you need to get started.

  1. The Clubs
    • Driver (1-wood) — for long tee shots
    • 3-wood or 5-wood — for fairway shots
    • Irons (5-9) — for approach shots
    • Pitching wedge & sand wedge — for chipping and bunkers
    • Putter — for the green
  2. The Gear
    • Golf bag — to carry your clubs
    • Golf balls (buy in bulk when starting out!)
    • Golf glove — improves grip, especially in heat
    • Golf shoes — spiked or spikeless for traction
    • Tees & ball markers
  3. The Etiquette
    • Repair divots and ball marks on the green
    • Rake bunkers after playing from them
    • Keep up with the pace of play
    • Silence your phone during others' shots
